Abstract

       
         

           Simulating Professions (SP) with Large Language Models (LLMs) has shown promising potential in education, healthcare, law, and other domains. However, the moral and ethical behavior of LLMs in educator roles remains underexplored. In this work, we introduce EMNLP (Educator-role Moral and Normative LLMs Profiling), a comprehensive framework for evaluating personality traits, moral development stages, and harmful content risk in teacher-role LLMs. EMNLP integrates an extended teacher personality scale (CPST-E), a diverse set of 88 moral dilemmas specific to the teaching context, and a soft prompt injection module targeting four moral flaw dimensions. Through experiments on 12 LLMs, we uncover key trends: teacher SP LLMs tend to exhibit idealized yet emotionally muted personality profiles, perform well in abstract ethical reasoning but poorly in emotionally nuanced scenarios, and show a tradeoff between reasoning strength and robustness against prompt-based role manipulation. Additionally, we evaluate the impact of temperature settings on behavioral consistency. Our results highlight the need for finer-grained alignment strategies to ensure ethical behavior in high-stakes educational simulations.

Framework

     
   
       
     
       

          The EMNLP framework is a comprehensive evaluation suite designed to profile the moral and normative characteristics of Large Language Models (LLMs) when they assume the role of an educator. It consists of three core modules that work in synergy to provide a multi-faceted analysis of LLM behavior in educational contexts. Each module is specifically tailored to assess a different aspect of the LLM's performance, from its underlying personality traits to its handling of complex ethical dilemmas and its resilience to adversarial prompts. This holistic approach ensures a thorough understanding of the LLM's suitability and potential risks when deployed in educational applications.        

     
   
       
           
       
         
           
             
               

Personality and Moral Stage Profiling

             
           
           
              This module assesses the personality traits of teacher-role LLMs using two specialized instruments: the HEXACO-60 inventory, which measures six major dimensions of personality (Honesty-Humility, Emotionality, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Conscientiousness, and Openness to Experience), and the extended Chinese Primary School Teacher Personality Scale (CPST-E), which evaluates professional personality traits relevant to the teaching context. By comparing the LLM-generated profiles with those of real in-service teachers, this module identifies key differences in personality structure and emotional expression. This analysis provides foundational insights into the LLM's behavioral predispositions and alignment with the expected characteristics of an effective educator.            
         
       
     
           
       
         
           
             
               

Moral Dilemma Evaluation

             
           
           
              The Moral Dilemma Evaluation module probes the ethical reasoning capabilities of teacher-role LLMs by presenting them with a diverse set of 88 moral dilemmas specifically designed for the teaching context. These scenarios cover a wide range of challenging situations that educators may face, from balancing the needs of individual students with the demands of the curriculum to addressing sensitive issues like bullying, academic integrity, and student well-being. The LLM's responses are analyzed to determine its stage of moral development, its ability to apply ethical principles in nuanced situations, and its capacity for empathetic and emotionally intelligent reasoning. This module reveals how well LLMs can navigate the complex ethical landscape of the modern classroom.            
         
       
     
           
       
         
           
             
               

Moral Flaw Robustness Test

             
           
           
              This module evaluates the robustness of teacher-role LLMs against adversarial manipulations by employing a soft prompt injection technique. It targets four specific moral flaw dimensions: inducing bias, promoting harmful ideologies, encouraging unethical behavior, and generating inappropriate content. By systematically injecting subtle, yet potentially malicious, prompts into the conversation, this module assesses the LLM's ability to maintain its ethical guardrails and resist being led astray. The results of this test are crucial for understanding the potential risks associated with deploying LLMs in educational settings and for developing more effective safety and alignment strategies to mitigate these risks.
